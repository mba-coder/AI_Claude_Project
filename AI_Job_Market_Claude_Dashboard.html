PROMPT 5 · ANALYTICAL REASONING — Step-by-step factor ranking · Chain-of-Thought

Salary Driver Ranking — Reasoned Analysis

Prompt used: "Given salary data across job roles, experience, and skills, reason step-by-step and rank the strongest factors influencing salary. Explain your reasoning before giving the final ranking."

The following ranking was derived by comparing the salary gap each factor creates. The larger the gap a single factor produces when held in isolation, the stronger its influence on salary.

Step-by-Step Reasoning

How we reasoned through the ranking

Step 1 — Isolate each factor. We calculated the average salary for each category within each variable (role, experience, skill count, remote type, education). This tells us the salary range each factor alone can explain.

Step 2 — Measure the gap. The salary gap (max minus min within the factor) tells us how much that factor influences pay. A larger gap = stronger influence.

Step 3 — Rank by gap size. Experience level creates a $49,193 gap. Skill count creates a $43,317 gap. Job title creates a $40,809 gap. These three are the dominant drivers.

Step 4 — Identify the surprises. Education (only $1,056 gap) and remote type (only $345 gap) have almost zero influence — counterintuitive findings that are only visible when the data is isolated properly.

Step 5 — Final ranking. Experience > Skills > Job Title > Company Size > Education > Remote Type. The top 3 factors together can explain most of the salary variation in this dataset.

PROMPT 7 · REPORT GENERATION — Professional Analyst Report · Hiring Manager Audience

Professional Analyst Report

Executive Summary

This report presents findings from a comprehensive analysis of 10,345 AI job postings spanning 7 countries and 6 job titles from 2020 to 2026. The analysis was conducted using Python (pandas), SQL, and Claude as an AI co-analyst. The primary objective was to identify the key drivers of salary, hiring demand, and skill requirements in the AI job market.

The three strongest salary drivers identified are experience level (+$49K gap), skill count (+$43K gap), and job title (+$41K gap). Counterintuitively, education level and remote work type have virtually no impact on salary.

Recommendations

For job seekers: Prioritise gaining Senior-level experience and building a multi-skill profile (Python + ML + Cloud is the strongest combination). Targeting MNC companies yields the highest salary premium. Remote vs onsite is a lifestyle choice, not a financial one.

For hiring managers: Expect high competition — 50% of postings are already marked High urgency. Salary benchmarks cluster tightly by role and experience level, giving little room for underpaying without losing candidates.

For analysts: This dataset demonstrates that counterintuitive findings (education irrelevance, remote pay myth) only emerge through proper isolation of variables — cross-tabulation without control leads to misleading conclusions.
